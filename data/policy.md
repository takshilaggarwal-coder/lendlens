POL-1.1 Scope — This program covers secured loans against used passenger vehicles sourced through partner dealers or direct sellers. Loan amounts from ₹1,00,000 to ₹12,00,000, tenures 12 to 60 months. All applications require complete KYC, income documentation, and a minimum three-month bank statement before any credit decision is recorded.

POL-3.2 Employment stability — Salaried applicants must show at least 12 months of continuous employment with the current employer, evidenced by an employer letter and matching salary credits. Tenure below the floor is a referable exception; it cannot be waived by branch staff and requires credit-manager sign-off with documented justification.

POL-4.1 Minimum income — The program floor is ₹20,000 net monthly income as derived from bank credits, not from stated figures. Files where derived income sits below the floor are declined regardless of collateral quality, co-applicant strength, or dealer relationship.

POL-4.2 Income verification — Stated take-home income must reconcile with salary credits observed in banking. A drift greater than 25% between stated and bank-derived income is a high-severity exception: the file cannot proceed on stated income and must be re-underwritten on the bank-derived figure with the discrepancy documented.

POL-4.3 Self-employed income proof — Proprietors must provide the latest income tax return plus banking that supports claimed drawings. Where the latest ITR is unavailable, derived business surplus from collections minus visible business outflows is used, and the file carries a mandatory conditions layer such as reduced LTV or a co-applicant.

POL-5.1 Repayment capacity (FOIR) — The fixed-obligation-to-income ratio, computed as all existing EMIs plus the proposed EMI divided by net monthly income, must not exceed 55%. Obligations are taken from banking NACH/ACH debits, not solely from declared liabilities. Breaches are high-severity and route to refer.

POL-5.3 Banking conduct — More than 2 inward returns or bounces in the last 12 months is a high-severity exception. Return charges, insufficient-funds markers, and reversal fees all count. Clean conduct with isolated single bounces may proceed with a note.

POL-6.2 Loan-to-value grid — Maximum LTV against the surveyor or quotation value: 90% for vehicles up to 3 years old, 80% for 4–7 years, 70% for 8 years and older. LTV breaches are medium severity and are typically cured by reducing sanctioned amount rather than declining.

POL-7.1 Fraud indicators — Watch for: perfectly round recurring salary credits, cash deposits shortly before salary-like transfers, the selling dealer doubling as the applicant's employer, shared phone numbers or addresses across unrelated applications, and employment letters signed by proprietors who are also the referring dealer. Any two indicators, or any cross-file linkage, routes the file to the fraud desk before credit review.

POL-8.1 Fair lending — Religion, caste, community, gender, and marital status are excluded from decisioning and must not appear in credit notes, reason codes, or pricing. Files are assessed on repayment capacity, banking conduct, employment stability, and collateral only. Violations are reportable compliance events.

POL-9.1 Manual review and reason codes — Every refer or decline must carry machine-readable reason codes tied to the specific policy section breached, with evidence references to the underlying document segments. Unexplained overrides of system recommendations are audited monthly.
